RFC Review Checklist:

1. Problem Statement
- Is the problem clearly stated?
- Is the business or technical impact measurable?
- Are affected stakeholders identified?

2. Proposed Solution
- Is the solution clearly described?
- Are main components and integration points explained?
- Are alternatives and trade-offs documented?

3. Security
- Is authentication defined?
- Is authorization defined?
- Is PII handling explained?
- Is encryption at rest and in transit covered?
- Is secrets management described?
- Is a threat model included?

4. Scalability
- Are expected RPS, peak load, users, or data volume specified?
- Is scaling strategy described?
- Are bottlenecks identified?
- Are SLOs defined?

5. Reliability
- Are retry, timeout, circuit breaker, and failure handling strategies defined?
- Is idempotency covered where retries are used?
- Is disaster recovery or backup approach mentioned where applicable?

6. Observability
- Are metrics defined?
- Are RED metrics covered: Rate, Errors, Duration?
- Are logs structured?
- Are traces/correlation IDs included?
- Are alert thresholds, dashboards, and runbooks defined?

7. Rollback
- Is rollback procedure defined?
- Is rollback owner named?
- Is rollback time estimated?
- Is data migration rollback considered?

8. Dependencies
- Are internal and external dependencies listed?
- Are SLA and failure impact of dependencies described?

9. Cost and Operations
- Are infrastructure and third-party costs estimated?
- Is cost monitoring defined?
- Is support ownership clear?
- Is on-call impact mentioned?
